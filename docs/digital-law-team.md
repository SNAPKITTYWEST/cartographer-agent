# Digital Law Team

The CARTO stack now carries a three-part kernel layer over one shared corpus:

- `Prolog` for legal reasoning and standing
- `COBOL` for procedural legal business operations
- `REXX` for business automation and docket glue

## Why These Two

### COBOL
Best for:
- corporate chunks
- fixed procedural flows
- ledgers
- statements
- notices
- account-class operations
- institution-shaped data movement

### REXX
Best for:
- scriptable office automation
- docket flow
- host integration
- repetitive legal operations
- parsing and transforming business text

## Team Shape

- `CARTO / Prolog Kernel` — sovereign legal terrain mapper and standing engine
- `COBOL Law Kernel` — procedural corporate chunk / claim / ledger / notice kernel
- `REXX Glue Kernel` — automation and workflow glue kernel

## Shared Corpus, Different Logic

All three kernels use the same CARTO corpus. They do not think the same way over it.

- `Prolog` pulls doctrine, standing, breach, and legal terrain
- `COBOL` pulls corporate chunks, records procedure, trust-account routines, notices, and fixed-shape institutional output
- `REXX` pulls docket motion, routing steps, text reshaping, and glue between repeated office actions

## Reasoning Fabric Above The Kernels

Above the kernel team sits a reasoning fabric:

- `CARTO` audits
- `REXX` routes and summons
- `Nemotron` attaches to engines and synthesizes
- `Gemini News` supplies current law and article context
- `Tokio FSM` controls the lifecycle from attach to seal

## Trust Deed Layer

Above the reasoning fabric sits the trust deed layer.

- It generates deeds on demand
- It governs summon and spawn authority
- It binds corporate creation to explicit deed scope
- It blocks runtime overrides of trustee or deed structure

## Routing Rule

- If the work is law + ledger + procedure: use `COBOL`
- If the work is law + automation + operator scripting: use `REXX`
- If the work is standing / breach / doctrine / reasoning: use `Prolog`
